-----------------------------------------
TYPE 1 — PARAGRAPH STUDY
-----------------------------------------

Breathing involves two stages: inspiration during which
atmospheric air is drawn in and expiration by which the alveolar
air is released out. The movement of air into and out of the lungs
is carried out by creating a pressure gradient between the lungs
and the atmosphere.

Inspiration can occur if the pressure within the lungs
(intra-pulmonary pressure) is less than the atmospheric pressure.
Expiration takes place when the intra-pulmonary pressure is
higher than the atmospheric pressure.

The diaphragm and a specialised set of muscles — external and
internal intercostals between the ribs, help in generation of such
gradients.

Inspiration is initiated by the contraction of diaphragm which
increases the volume of thoracic chamber in the antero-posterior
axis. The contraction of external inter-costal muscles lifts up the
ribs and the sternum causing an increase in the volume of the
thoracic chamber in the dorso-ventral axis. The overall increase
in thoracic volume causes a similar increase in pulmonary volume.
An increase in pulmonary volume decreases the intra-pulmonary
pressure to less than atmospheric pressure which forces air from
outside to move into the lungs — inspiration.

Relaxation of the diaphragm and inter-costal muscles returns
them to normal positions and reduces thoracic volume and thereby
pulmonary volume. This leads to increase in intra-pulmonary
pressure to slightly above atmospheric pressure causing expulsion
of air from lungs — expiration.

On an average, a healthy human breathes 12-16 times/minute.
Volume of air involved in breathing can be estimated by a
spirometer which helps in clinical assessment of pulmonary
functions.

-----------------------------------------
TYPE 2 — POINTER NOTES STUDY
-----------------------------------------

MECHANISM:
- Breathing = two stages: INSPIRATION and EXPIRATION
- Inspiration = atmospheric air drawn IN
- Expiration = alveolar air released OUT
- Movement of air = by creating PRESSURE GRADIENT between
  lungs and atmosphere

INSPIRATION:
- Intra-pulmonary pressure < Atmospheric pressure
- Diaphragm CONTRACTS → thoracic volume increases
  (antero-posterior axis)
- External intercostal muscles CONTRACT → ribs and sternum
  lift up → thoracic volume increases (dorso-ventral axis)
- Overall thoracic volume increases → pulmonary volume increases
- Intra-pulmonary pressure decreases below atmospheric pressure
- Air moves IN from outside = INSPIRATION

EXPIRATION:
- Intra-pulmonary pressure > Atmospheric pressure
- Diaphragm and intercostal muscles RELAX
- Thoracic volume decreases → pulmonary volume decreases
- Intra-pulmonary pressure increases above atmospheric pressure
- Air expelled OUT = EXPIRATION

KEY FACTS:
- Additional abdominal muscles help increase strength of
  inspiration and expiration
- Healthy human breathes 12-16 times/minute
- Spirometer = estimates volume of air in breathing movements
- Spirometer used for clinical assessment of pulmonary functions

RESPIRATORY VOLUMES:
- Tidal Volume (TV) = volume inspired or expired in normal
  respiration = approx 500 mL
- Healthy man can inspire/expire 6000-8000 mL per minute
- IRV (Inspiratory Reserve Volume) = extra air inspired by
  forcible inspiration = 2500-3000 mL
- ERV (Expiratory Reserve Volume) = extra air expired by
  forcible expiration = 1000-1100 mL
- RV (Residual Volume) = air remaining after forcible expiration
  = 1100-1200 mL

PULMONARY CAPACITIES:
- IC (Inspiratory Capacity) = TV + IRV
- EC (Expiratory Capacity) = TV + ERV
- FRC (Functional Residual Capacity) = ERV + RV
- VC (Vital Capacity) = ERV + TV + IRV
- TLC (Total Lung Capacity) = RV + ERV + TV + IRV
  = VC + RV

-----------------------------------------
TYPE 3 — TRICKS & MNEMONICS
-----------------------------------------

Mnemonic for Respiratory Volumes:
"Tall Indians Earn Respect"
T - Tidal Volume (TV) = 500 mL
I - IRV = 2500-3000 mL
E - ERV = 1000-1100 mL
R - RV = 1100-1200 mL

Mnemonic for Pulmonary Capacities:
"I Eat Fresh Vegetables Today"
I - IC = TV + IRV
E - EC = TV + ERV
F - FRC = ERV + RV
V - VC = ERV + TV + IRV
T - TLC = RV + ERV + TV + IRV

Trick for Inspiration vs Expiration:
INSPIRATION = Contract → Volume UP → Pressure DOWN → Air IN
EXPIRATION = Relax → Volume DOWN → Pressure UP → Air OUT
