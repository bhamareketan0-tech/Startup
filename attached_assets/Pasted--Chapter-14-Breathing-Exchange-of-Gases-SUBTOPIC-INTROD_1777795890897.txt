TYPE 1 — PARAGRAPH STUDY
-----------------------------------------

Read the following passage carefully:

Oxygen (O2) is utilised by the organisms to indirectly break down
simple molecules like glucose, amino acids, fatty acids, etc., to derive
energy to perform various activities. Carbon dioxide (CO2) which is
harmful is also released during the above catabolic reactions.
It is therefore evident that O2 has to be continuously provided to
the cells and CO2 produced by the cells have to be released out.
This process of exchange of O2 from the atmosphere with CO2
produced by the cells is called breathing, commonly known as
respiration. Place your hands on your chest; you can feel the chest
moving up and down. You know that it is due to breathing.
The respiratory organs and the mechanism of breathing are
described in the following sections of this chapter.

-----------------------------------------
TYPE 2 — POINTER NOTES STUDY
-----------------------------------------

Key pointers from Introduction:

- O2 is used INDIRECTLY to break down simple molecules
- Simple molecules = glucose, amino acids, fatty acids
- Purpose = derive energy for various activities
- CO2 is HARMFUL — released during catabolic reactions
- O2 must be CONTINUOUSLY supplied to cells
- CO2 produced must be RELEASED OUT
- Exchange of O2 (atmosphere) with CO2 (cells) = BREATHING
- Breathing is commonly known as RESPIRATION
- Physical sign of breathing = chest moves UP and DOWN
- Respiratory organs and mechanism = described in FOLLOWING SECTIONS

E - Exchange = O2 in, CO2 out = Breathing
